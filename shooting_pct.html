Shooting Percentages

The ability to put the ball in the basket is, understandably, the most important statistic in basketball. No matter what else a team does in a game, the most points wins. This leads to many measures of shooting percentage to describe a team's skill.

Free Throw Percentage measures only free throws, the only undefended play in basketball. Typically, you would expect that the teams that best take advantage of these 'free' points would win the highest percentage of their games, but that isn't always the case. Additionally, it is valuable to pair this measure with free throw attempt rate (on the 'Pace' page) to more accurately picture a team's free throw situation.

Field Goal Percentage describes the success rate at which a team makes all shots except free throws. This is a statistic that equally counts two point attempts and three point attempts. This is a good, though basic, statistic for describing a team's ability to shoot the basketball, score points, and therefore win games.

Two Point Percentage is nearly identical to field goal percentage but only accounts for shots taken inside the three point arc. This percentage should be higher than both field goal percentage and three point percentage as it is describing the 'easier' shots.

Three Point Percentage, much like two point percentage, is a subset of field goal percentage. In this case, it measures all shots taken from beyond the three point line. Note that this would even include full court desperation shots. Converse to two point percentage, this is typically lower than both field goal percentage and two point percentage because of the shot distance and difficulty.

Effective Field Goal Percentage is our first 'advanced' shooting statistic. Much like field goal percentage, this statistic accounts for all non free throws, but does not weight two pointers and three pointers equally. It is calculated by the formula: eFG% = (FGM + 0.5 * 3PM) / FGA. Theoretically, this equation gives an offense credit for the increased value of the three point shot in their field goal percentage. Effective field goal percentage will never be lower than field goal percentage.

True Shooting Percentage is perhaps the best measure of scoring efficacy, represented by the equation: TS% = PTS / (2 * (FGA + 0.44 * FTA)). This accounts for all scoring oppurtunities in an attempt to describe scoring efficiency. True shooting percentage should be a similar, though slightly higher, number than eFG% because of the inclusion of free throws. It also wins the prize for the highest correlation with win percentage of any single statistic.
