Rebound Percentages

Unlike all the other displayed statistics, these measures of rebounding success are calculated through my own algorithms. These represent the percentage at which a team successfully rebounds on each end of the floor. While rebounding does not earn any points, gaining possessions gives teams more chances to score and takes that opportunity away from their opponent.

Offensive Rebound Success Percentage represents the percentage of rebounds a team got on the offensive end of the floor. In other words, the percentage of the time a team rebounded their own missed shot. This is calculated by dividing the number of offensive rebounds a team pulled in by the total number of rebounds available while they were on offense.

Defensive Rebound Success Percentage is the converse measure to ORS%, representing the percentage of available rebounds on defense that a team possessed. This is typically a much higher percenteage than ORS%, as players are in a much better rebounding position on defense.

Total Rebound Success Percentage is calculated simply by summing ORS% and DRS%, meaning that there is a maximum value of 200%. A value of 100% would mean that a team got exactly half of the available rebounds in their games. This is average. Any team above 104% is in the top quarter of teams nationally. The average win percentage for these teams is 13% higher than the national average for all teams, exemplifying the benefit of successfully rebounding at a high percentage.
