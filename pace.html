Pace

Each visualization on this page describes the frequency in which a specific event occurs. These statistics do not typically correlate well with win percentage, but they are extremely important in understanding how a team plays and properly assessing their strength relative to other teams that may play a different style of basketball.

Pace describes the average number of possessions per game. Pace is important to adjust for to account for defensive teams. For example, the 2019 NCAA Champion Virginia Cavaliers averaged only 70.4 points per game, which was 211th in the nation. However, they ran the slowest offense in the country and had one of the best defenses, proving the need to account for pace.

Free Throw Attempt Rate is the number of free throw attempts per field goal attempt. This is not necessarily a good indicator for a team's success largely because it does not account for the team's ability to make either free throws or field goals.

Three Point Attempt Rate, similar to free throw attempt rate, is the number of three point attempts per field goal attempt. This is an interesting statistic as three point attmpts do count as field goal attempts as well, so this measure should read as the percentage of field goals that were three pointers, rather than as a ratio of three pointers to two pointers.

Free Throws per Field Goal Attempt is nearly the same as free throw attemp rate, though it only counts made free throws, making it a significantly better marker for success. This is exemplified by the significantly higher R-value, though neither statistic is especially indicative on their own.
